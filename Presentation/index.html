Research Introduction


  • Impossible to analyse Financial Market analytically due to complex dynamics

  • Interactions between heterogenous agents generate various emergent phenomena

  • Agent-Based Model (ABM) captures heterogeneity at micro-level

  • We use the Genoa ABM to analyse effect of cooperation on various emergent phenomena

Gopikrishnan et al., 1999

Scaling of the distribution of fluctuations of financial market indices

Historically, return distribution was assumed to follow Gaussian distribution.

However, empirical analysis showed pronounced tails!

So, Gopikrishnan et al. performed an analysis of the return distribution of the S&P 500 index with 3 different time scales: minutes, days and years.


What did they find?
Their findings

Their findings

Power law asymptotic behaviour

For time periods up to 4 days, the probability of returns are consistent with a power-law.

.. with exponent ≈ 3!

This lies well outside stable Levy regime 0 > a > 2.

Gaussian for longer time periods

For time periods longer than 4 days, the returns slowly converge to a Gaussian ditribution.

Raberto et al., 2001

Agent-based simulation of a financial market

ABM model of the financial market, called the Genoa Market Model.

This Genoa Market model is able to reproduce the leptokurtic shape of the probability density of logarithmic price returns,
i.e the power-law distribution we have observed before.

What does this model look like?
Genoa Market Model

Genoa Market Model

An overview of the elements and dynamics of the model.

Agents (traders) get initialised with an fixed amount of money

There is no explicit money creation during the model's lifetime.

Clustering with probability Pc

Two traders get paired and create a small cluster. If one of the traders already belongs to a cluster, their clusters merge.

Agent either places sell or buy order with equal probability

Both sell and buy orders are constrained by limit prices. These limit prices depend on the previous asset price and a random number drawn from N(1.01, σ), where σ is the historical volatility.

Cluster activation with probability Pa

One random cluster gets chosen in case of cluster activation. All agents in cluster behave the same and thus all either create buy or sell orders.

Clearing price is determined by the intersection of demand and supply curves

All buy orders with limit price lower than clearing price, get cancelled. The opposite happens for sell orders.

Experiment

Research Question:

What is the effect of changing the level of cooperation (i.e, probability with which agents are willing to work together) on the return distribution in the Genoa model?

Hypothesis:

We expect that without cooperation the price returns over time resemble Gaussian noise and no power law appears.

With extreme cooperation, we expect to observe extreme inflation and market crashes due to extreme homogeneous behaviour.

In between, we expect the power law to appear with the exponent value depending on the level of clustering.
